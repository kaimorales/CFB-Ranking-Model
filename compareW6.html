Outcome Validation (Week 6)

The clearest test this week: the model identified two AP Top-10 teams as over-ranked before kickoff—and both lost to AP-unranked opponents:

  • Texas (AP #9): lost to Florida.
  • Penn State (AP #7): lost to UCLA.

That’s exactly the aim of a wins-first system: reward teams for what they’ve done (ranked wins at the time, strong opponents, SOS) and down-weight brand reputation. Week-of-game opponent quality (via Coaches Poll) ensures upsets are valued when they happened—not retroactively.

Why the Model Outperforms Poll Heuristics

  • Week-specific quality: opponent strength \(Q\) is taken from the Coaches Poll at game time, not end-of-season hindsight.
  • Ranked-win emphasis: multiplicative boost for ranked wins and discounted penalties for ranked losses.
  • Conference tiering for unranked: unranked P4 wins > unranked G5 wins; G5 losses sting more.
  • SOS scaling: season-level multiplier favors teams who schedule (and beat) stronger opponents.
  • Diminishing returns: curbs padding against unranked G5 teams.

Result: teams like Georgia Tech and Vanderbilt get properly credited for real wins, while teams elevated by reputation alone (e.g., Texas, Penn State) are not protected.
